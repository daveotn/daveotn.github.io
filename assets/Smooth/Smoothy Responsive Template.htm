The Real World

Looking away from our prism of screens from our caves, we can see the real world. Not on replay; not scripted; not with commercial breaks or ads.

1

Imagine a cave where those inside never see the outside world. Instead, they see shadows of that world projected on the cave wall.

2

The world they see in the shadows is not the real world, but it’s real to them. If you would show the world as it actually is, they would instead reject it as incomprehensible.

3

Now, what if you were outside in the world, but you couldn’t see it because you weren’t looking? Because you trusted that the world you saw through the prism was the real world.

4

But there’s a difference. You see, unlike the allegory of the cave where the shadows are false, there are other people.

5

This is the delusion of the narcissist who believes that they alone are real. Their feelings are the only feelings that matter because other people are shadows...

6

...and shadows don’t feel, because they’re not real.

But what if everyone lived in caves; then no one would be real, not even you.

7

Unless one day you woke up and left the cave.

8

How strange would the world look after a lifetime of looking at shadows?
